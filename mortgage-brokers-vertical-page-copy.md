## 1. Quick layout review

**What works**

- 11-section flow mirrors the dental page architecturally — same system, different content. Cross-page system credibility.
- The differentiating insight from the brief is preserved: **HomeCalc is same-vertical proof, not adjacent-industry bridge.** Section 2 (real query block) and Section 5 (same-vertical framing) are the two highest-leverage sections on this page. Both are doing work the dental page can't do.
- First paragraph after H1 carries the AI Overviews extraction (R2).
- Comparison table is built around the actual three options a Canadian mortgage broker is weighing (audit / mortgage SEO agency / lead gen platform — Ratehub/LowestRates named), per R3.
- YMYL discipline maintained — page is about *the broker's marketing visibility*, never about mortgage rates, qualification math, or anything that would create a YMYL liability for HamiTahm.com.

**What should change before build**

- **Swap Sections 10 and 11.** Same convention — Related Reading before Final CTA so booking is the close.
- **One placeholder card in Section 5, not two.** Same call as the dental page. Two placeholders on a vertical page read as scaffolding; one reads as honest work-in-progress.
- **Section 2 query block must render as text, not a graphic.** This is the AEO extraction crown jewel — real Canadian homebuyer queries, real citation numbers, on a real mortgage site. If a designer renders this as an image, the entire ranking advantage evaporates.
- **Power statement under hero paragraph, not above it.** Same reasoning as the AEO/GEO/dental pages — preserve the first paragraph as the clean extraction anchor.

**Fill-ins required before launch**
1. Walkthrough call duration (assumed 60 minutes — matches the rest of the system).
2. Turnaround SLA (assumed 7 business days — matches the system).
3. Month label on the placeholder mortgage brokerage case study.
4. Confirm the queries and citation counts in Section 2 and Section 5 against the actual HomeCalc dashboard — I used the figures from the brief, but a few queries are listed across both this brief and the HomeCalc case study brief and the citation counts should match across pages.
