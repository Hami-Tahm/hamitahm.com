## 3. SEO/AEO/GEO checks

**Primary keyword usage** — `ai visibility` appears in: H1, recommended title, recommended meta, first paragraph, multiple H2s (definition, services, resources), FAQ, CTA. Natural density across a hub-style page.

**Supporting keyword usage**
- `ai visibility analysis` — natural in the proof section or the definition trim.
- `stay visible in the age of ai search` — slot into hero subheading or intro if needed; otherwise leave out. Not worth forcing.
- `generative ai impact brand visibility` and `ai era brand visibility` — work into the consultant-wedge intro or the definition block if they land naturally.
- `boost local visibility with ai` — supported by Toronto/Canada framing throughout. Not used as a direct phrase.
- `visible ai` — **caution.** Likely a branded product. Do not use this phrase in copy; let it surface only through natural use of "visibility" + "AI" nearby.

**Cannibalization** — Managed if the definition trim happens.
- Hub owns `ai visibility` broad, `ai visibility canada`, `ai visibility toronto`, `ai visibility services canada`.
- `/blog/what-is-ai-visibility/` owns the definition intent — hub yields by trimming and linking out.
- `/ai-visibility/ai-visibility-audit/` owns transactional terms — hub never targets those.
- AEO and GEO consultant pages each own their respective keyword — hub references and links, doesn't compete.

**Topical authority** — The Services Directory + Resources section + ProfessionalService schema with `hasOfferCatalog` is what makes this page read as a real hub to both readers and Google. Schema names every child service explicitly, so even if a child page is slow to index, Google sees the topical structure from the hub.

**Local SEO** — Toronto and Canada appear in: first paragraph, consultant wedge, schema `areaServed`. Combined with the future Google Business Profile (out of scope here, flagged), this is the local-pack play against dNOVO Group and Chief AI Advisors.

**Cannibalization with the homepage** — None. Homepage is navigational (Hami Tahm brand). Hub is informational (AI visibility topic). They never target the same query.
